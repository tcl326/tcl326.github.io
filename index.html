About Me

I major in Mechanical Engineering at NYU Abu Dhabi. I am intrigued by robot autonomy, environmental sustainability, and user-centered design. I am constantly exploring the applications of robotics for the most pressing social problems of our generation.

Currently, I am engaged in the emerging field of neural networks and robot autonomy. I believe this aspect of machine learning will enable the formulation of robots neccesary to confront pragmatically the complexity and the unpredictability that pervade the real world.

Motion Based River Segmentation

Identifying river shoreline is essential in the development of autonomous surface vehicles (self-driving boats). Using the OpenCV library, I track the motion of features and calculate the entropy or complexity of the feature motion. Since feature motion of features on river will exhibit higher entropy than those on land due to wave motion, I can identify the location of river region and of land region.

  • Organisation: Dynamical Systems Laboratory
  • Date: Ongoing
  • Key Techniques: OpenCV, Computer Vision

Wadi Drone

Wadi Drone is the award winning drone of The 2015 UAE Drones for Good competition, winning 1 Million AED. Wadi Drone is a conservation drone that facilitates the collection of hard to reach data, such as the wildlife photo captured by camera traps located in the heart of a mountain range. In this project I developed the communication and data transfer systems between the data capturing device and the drone. Additionally I also participated in the design process and design the power system of the drone.

  • Organisation: Wadi Drone
  • Date: Ongoing
  • Key Techniques: Arduino, Raspberry Pi, Prototyping, Autopilot, Raspberry Pi, Wireless Protocols

Hakeeme

Hakeeme is the winning hack of The 2015 Annual NYUAD International Hackathon for Social Good in the Arab World that aims to improve the migrant labour situation in the Arab World. Hakeeme is an armband that would keep track of worker vital signs like pulse and hydration levels. By providing the live feed of this data, companies and government can easily track the conditions of workers and prevent unnecessary death. Furthermore, this armband can also keep the construction companies liable by reporting violations such as working during the noon.

  • Organisation: Hakeeme
  • Date: April 2015
  • Key Techniques: Arduino, 3D Printing

Flexural Rigidity of Cornstalk

Current method of measuring cornstalk strength have difficulty in differentiating cornstalk strength above certain threshold. I designed a proof of concept prototype that would be able to measure the strength of cornstalk more accurately by measuring its flexural rigidity. Using pressure sensor and IMU, I was able to fuse the data and obtain the flexural rigidity of the cornstalk.

  • Organisation: NYUAD Biomechanics Laboratory
  • Date: July 2015
  • Key Techniques: Arduino, 3D Printing, Prototyping, Data Fusion

Asthma Training Wheel

For the final project of the course, Design and Innovation, we were required to create a device that would improve the living standard or alleviate the life of asthma patient. After reading the blog of a mother of asthmatic child, who worried constantly about the condition of her child, we decided to create device that would help to alleviate that using human centric design. We designed and created a device that would allow the parent to know if the child has the inhaler with them and in case of asthma attack, if the child used the inhaler and whether or not the child is safe.

  • Date: January 2014
  • Key Techniques: Arduino, Soft Sensors, Prototyping

Eccentric Sphere Gear Papercraft

This is the papercraft inspired by KAMIKARA's Heart Gear Papercraft . I was really fascinated by the seemly random motion exhibited by the moving gears. To imitate the seemly random motion, I created a sphere gear with a shifted center. This makes the gear to break apart from a sphere and exhibit interesting motion and return to being a sphere after one full rotation. You can watch my papercraft in action from here.

  • Date: May 2012
  • Key Techniques: Papercrafting, 3D modeling
